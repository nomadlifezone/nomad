# Complete Shanghai Travel Guide

Shanghai, China's most cosmopolitan metropolis, blends Eastern and Western cultures. From the century-old Bund to modern skyscrapers, from traditional alleys to fashionable districts, this guide helps you explore the magic of Shanghai.

## 🗺️ Must-Visit Attractions

### 1. The Bund ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Features**: Shanghai's iconic landmark, gallery of international architecture

**Recommended Visit**:
- Best time: Evening 5-8 PM (sunset and night view)
- Viewing spots: Bund promenade, Peace Hotel rooftop
- Duration: 2-3 hours

**Admission**: Free

**Transportation**: Metro Line 2/10, East Nanjing Road Station

### 2. Oriental Pearl Tower / Shanghai Tower ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Oriental Pearl Tower**:
- Admission: ¥160-220 (different observation decks)
- Feature: Classic Shanghai landmark
- Height: 468 meters

**Shanghai Tower**:
- Admission: ¥180
- Feature: China's tallest building
- Height: 632 meters (observation deck at 546 meters)

**Recommendation**: Choose Shanghai Tower for more spectacular views

### 3. Yu Garden + City God Temple ⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Yu Garden**:
- Admission: ¥40
- Feature: Ming Dynasty classical garden
- Duration: 1.5 hours

**City God Temple**:
- Admission: ¥10
- Feature: Taoist temple, surrounding snack street

**Food**: Nanxiang Steamed Buns, pear syrup candy, spiced beans

### 4. Tianzifang ⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Features**: Art district converted from Shikumen alleys

**Recommended Activities**:
- Browse unique shops and galleries
- Taste creative coffee and snacks
- Photo opportunities
- Duration: 2-3 hours

**Admission**: Free
**Transportation**: Metro Line 9, Dapuqiao Station

### 5. Shanghai Disneyland ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Admission**: ¥399-799 (varies by season)
**Duration**: Full day (10+ hours)
**Must-Try Rides**:
- TRON Lightcycle Power Run ⚡
- Soaring Over the Horizon 🌍
- Pirates of the Caribbean ⚓
- Seven Dwarfs Mine Train ⛏️

**Money-Saving Tips**:
- Weekday tickets are cheaper
- Buy early bird tickets in advance
- Purchase early entry pass

### 6. Nanjing Road Pedestrian Street ⭐⭐⭐⭐

- China's most famous shopping street
- Major brands gathered
- Duration: 2-3 hours
- Night view is more beautiful

### 7. Xintiandi ⭐⭐⭐⭐

- Shikumen architectural style
- High-end dining and bars
- Best to visit at night
- Admission: Free

### 8. Zhujiajiao Water Town ⭐⭐⭐⭐

- Jiangnan water town style
- 1 hour from city center
- Admission: Free (some attractions charge)
- Duration: Half day

## 📅 Recommended Itinerary

### 3-Day Classic Route

**Day 1: The Bund + Nanjing Road**
- Morning: Yu Garden, City God Temple (3 hours)
- Noon: City God Temple snacks
- Afternoon: Nanjing Road shopping (2 hours)
- Evening: The Bund night view (2 hours)
- Dinner: Bund restaurant

**Day 2: Pudong + Modern Shanghai**
- Morning: Shanghai Tower observation deck (2 hours)
- Noon: Lujiazui area lunch
- Afternoon: Shanghai Museum or Science Museum (3 hours)
- Evening: Oriental Pearl area night view

**Day 3: Artistic Shanghai**
- Morning: Tianzifang (2 hours)
- Noon: Tianzifang area restaurants
- Afternoon: Xintiandi (2 hours)
- Evening: Huaihai Road shopping

### 5-Day In-Depth Route
Add to 3-day itinerary:

**Day 4: Shanghai Disneyland**
- Full day at Disneyland
- Evening fireworks show

**Day 5: Water Town + Leisure**
- Morning: Zhujiajiao water town (half day)
- Afternoon: Return to city, Jing'an Temple area
- Evening: Shaanxi South Road food street

## 🍜 Shanghai Cuisine

### Must-Try Foods

**1. Pan-Fried Buns** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- Recommended: Xiao Yang Shengjian, Da Hu Chun
- Price: ¥10-15/4 pieces
- Feature: Crispy golden bottom

**2. Soup Dumplings** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- Recommended: Nanxiang Mantou Dian, Jia Jia Tang Bao
- Price: ¥30-50/steamer
- Feature: Thin skin with rich soup

**3. Local Shanghai Cuisine**
- Red-braised pork, fermented tofu, oil-braised eel
- Recommended restaurants: Lao Zheng Xing, Shanghai Ren Jia
- Per person: ¥100-200

**4. Scallion Oil Noodles**
- Recommended: Aniang Noodles
- Price: ¥15-20
- Feature: Simple but delicious

**5. Pork Chop Rice Cake**
- Recommended: Xian De Lai
- Price: ¥25-35
- Feature: Traditional Shanghai snack

### Celebrity Restaurants
- **Ultraviolet** - 3 Michelin stars (reservation required)
- **8 1/2 Otto e Mezzo Bombana** - Italian cuisine
- **Mr & Mrs Bund** - French cuisine
- **Lost Heaven** - Yunnan cuisine

## 🚇 Transportation Guide

### Arriving in Shanghai

**Pudong Airport**:
- Maglev → Metro Line 2 (¥50 + metro fare)
- Airport bus (¥30, to various city locations)
- Taxi (¥150-200, 1 hour)

**Hongqiao Airport/Railway Station**:
- Metro Line 2/10 direct
- Taxi to city center ¥50-80

### City Transportation

**Metro** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Recommended)
- Comprehensive line coverage
- Single trip ¥3-10
- Use Alipay/WeChat QR code

**Taxi/Ride-hailing**
- Starting fare ¥14 (3 km)
- City taxi usually ¥20-50

**Shared Bikes**
- Hellobike, Meituan Bike
- ¥1.5-2 per hour

## ⚠️ Important Notes

### Safety Tips
- Crowded at the Bund, beware of pickpockets
- Don't trust street touts for "tea houses" or "bars"
- Avoid deserted alleys at night

### Best Travel Time
- **Spring (Mar-May)**: Comfortable weather ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Autumn (Sep-Nov)**: Pleasant climate ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Summer (Jun-Aug)**: Hot and rainy ⭐⭐⭐
- **Winter (Dec-Feb)**: Cold but fewer tourists ⭐⭐⭐

### Practical Tips
1. Download Shanghai Metro App and Dianping
2. Book popular restaurants in advance
3. Disneyland tickets need at least 1 day advance booking
4. Prepare small amount of cash (most places accept mobile payment)
5. The Bund is extremely crowded on weekends, visit on weekdays
