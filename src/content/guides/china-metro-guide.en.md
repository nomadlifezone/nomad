## 🎫 Ticket Purchase Methods

### Method 1: Single-Journey Ticket (Ticket Vending Machine) ⭐⭐⭐

**Purchase Process**:
1. Find destination station name at ticket vending machine
2. Tap screen to select destination (supports Chinese and English)
3. Select quantity (1-10 tickets)
4. Check fare
5. Payment:
   - Cash (bills/coins)
   - WeChat Pay (scan code)
   - Alipay (scan code)
6. Collect ticket and change

**Fare**: Typically ¥2-10 based on distance

**Pros**: Simple and straightforward
**Cons**: Need to buy each time, time-consuming

### Method 2: QR Code (Recommended) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Alipay QR Code**:
1. Open Alipay
2. Search "Metro Pass" or "QR Code" on homepage
3. Select your city
4. Activate QR code (authorize password-free payment)
5. Scan code when entering station
6. Scan again when exiting

**WeChat QR Code**:
1. Open WeChat
2. "Me" → "Services" → "Transportation"
3. Select "QR Code"
4. Choose city to activate
5. Same usage as Alipay

**Pros**:
- ✅ No need to queue for tickets
- ✅ Auto-deduct payment
- ✅ Usually discounted (95% off)
- ✅ Most convenient and fast

**Note**: Some older lines may not support

### Method 3: Transit Card ⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Purchase Locations**:
- Metro station service center
- Convenience stores (some cities)
- Metro automatic card machines

**Cost**:
- Card fee: ¥20-30 (deposit, refundable)
- Initial top-up: Starting from ¥50

**Top-up Methods**:
- Metro station automatic top-up machines
- Convenience stores (some cities)
- Bank branches

**Pros**:
- Can use on buses, taxis (some cities)
- Usually discounted (90-95% off)
- No need for phone network

**Major Transit Cards**:
- Beijing: Yikatong
- Shanghai: Transportation Card
- Guangzhou: Yangcheng Tong
- Shenzhen: Shenzhen Tong

### Method 4: Mobile NFC ⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Supported Devices**:
- Apple Pay (iPhone/Apple Watch)
- Huawei/Xiaomi/OPPO with NFC-enabled Android phones

**Activation**:
- Add transit card in phone wallet app
- Top-up online

**Pros**:
- No network needed
- Fast entry and exit
- Same convenience as physical card

## 🚇 Riding Process

### Step 1: Entering Station

1. **Security Check**:
   - Bags through X-ray machine
   - People walk through side passage
   - Prohibited items: Knives, lighters, alcohol, pets (except guide dogs)

2. **Tap Card/Scan Code at Gate**:
   - Single ticket: Place on yellow sensing area
   - QR code: Align with scanner
   - Transit card/NFC: Touch sensing area

3. **Gate Opens**:
   - Pass through quickly
   - Don't linger

### Step 2: Find Direction

1. Look at station signs (Chinese and English)
2. Confirm line and direction (e.g., "Line 1 toward Sihui East")
3. Go downstairs/escalator to platform

**Important**:
- 🔴 Red/dark = No entry
- 🟢 Green/white = Can pass
- Each direction has separate platform, wrong direction requires exiting and re-entering

### Step 3: Waiting for Train

1. Queue at platform (floor markings indicate queuing area)
2. Let passengers exit first, then board
3. Mind safety line (yellow line)

**Peak Hours**:
- Morning rush: 7:30-9:00
- Evening rush: 17:30-19:30

### Step 4: On the Train

1. Find seat or stand steady and hold on
2. Give priority seats to elderly, pregnant, children, disabled
3. Keep quiet (no loud phone calls)
4. No eating (prohibited in some cities)
5. Watch overhead/door route map and station display

### Step 5: Transferring (if needed)

1. Listen/watch for transfer station announcements
2. After getting off, follow "Line X Transfer" signs
3. No need to exit, walk directly to another line's platform
4. Some transfers require longer walks (5-10 minutes)

**Transfer-Friendly Cities**:
- Shanghai, Shenzhen: Well-designed transfers
- Beijing: Some transfers quite far

### Step 6: Exiting Station

1. Get off at destination
2. Follow "Exit" signs
3. Exit gates:
   - Single ticket: Insert into collection slot
   - QR code: Scan
   - Transit card/NFC: Tap

4. Choose correct exit (A/B/C/D, etc.)
5. Check exit signs for your destination

## 💰 Fares and Discounts

### Fare Calculation

- Base fare: ¥2-3 (starting price)
- Increases by distance: +¥1 every 5-10 km
- Generally within city: ¥2-7
- Cross-district: ¥8-15

### Discount Policies

| Group | Discount | Notes |
|-------|----------|-------|
| Children | Free/Half | Free under 1.3m height |
| Students | 50% off | Need student card |
| Elderly | Free/Half | 65+ (varies by city) |
| Disabled | Free | With disability certificate |

### QR Code Discounts

- Usually 95% off
- Some cities have promotional activities (like first ride free)

## ⚠️ Important Notes

### Safety Matters

- ❌ Don't cross yellow safety line
- ❌ Don't hold doors or lean on doors
- ❌ Watch belongings, beware pickpockets
- ✅ Be careful of crowds during rush hour
- ✅ Press red button in car for emergencies

### Prohibited Items

- 🚫 Smoking
- 🚫 Eating (strictly prohibited in some cities)
- 🚫 Carrying dangerous items
- 🚫 Begging, distributing flyers
- 🚫 Loud noise
- 🚫 Pets (except guide dogs)

### Cultural Etiquette

- Let passengers exit first
- Give seats to those in need
- Don't block doors
- Don't play music in cars
- Stand steady, don't sway around

### Practical Tips

1. **Leave early**: Rush hour is very crowded
2. **Avoid rush hours**: If possible
3. **Download map apps**: Won't get lost
4. **Remember hotel's nearby metro station**
5. **Take photos of station names**: Easy to find way back
6. **Note last train time**: Usually around 23:00

## 🕐 Operating Hours

- **Last train**: 22:30-23:30

**Check**:
- Timetables in metro stations

## 🆘 What to Do When Problems Arise

### Went Wrong Direction

- Get off at next station
- Cross to opposite platform and go back
- Or exit and re-enter (need new ticket/scan)

### Missed Your Stop

- Get off at next station
- Go back (no need to exit)

### Can't Find Exit

- Look at floor/wall "Exit" signs
- Ask staff (service desks at all stations)
- Use map app navigation

### Phone Dead/No Network

- Find ticket vending machine to buy single ticket
- Or borrow power bank from service desk

### Lost Items

- Immediately notify platform staff
- Call metro lost and found number
- Check official app/website
