# Complete Guide to Mobile and Internet for Foreigners in China

Mobile phones and internet access are essential when traveling or living in China. This guide helps you quickly set up SIM cards, network access, and essential apps.

## 📱 Buying a Chinese SIM Card

### Three Major Carriers Comparison

| Carrier | Features | Coverage | Price | Rating |
|---------|----------|----------|-------|--------|
| China Mobile | Best signal, most users | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ | Medium | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ |
| China Unicom | Good international roaming | ⭐⭐⭐⭐ | Lower | ⭐⭐⭐⭐ |
| China Telecom | Fast 4G/5G speeds | ⭐⭐⭐⭐ | Medium | ⭐⭐⭐⭐ |

### Where to Buy

**1. Airport Counters** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- Most convenient, buy upon arrival
- Slightly expensive but time-saving
- Staff will help with setup
- Price: ¥50-150 (7-30 day plans)

**2. Official Stores** ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- Affordable prices
- More plan options
- Requires passport
- May need Chinese address

**3. Convenience Stores** ⭐⭐⭐
- Prepaid SIM cards
- Self-activation
- Price: ¥30-100

### Recommended Plans

**Short-term Tourists (7-15 days)**:
- 5GB data + 50 min calls
- Price: ¥50-80

**Medium-term Stay (30 days)**:
- 20GB data + 100 min calls
- Price: ¥100-150

**Long-term Residents (Monthly)**:
- 30-100GB data + unlimited calls
- Price: ¥50-200/month

## 🌐 Internet Access

### Blocked Websites and Apps
The following cannot be accessed directly in China:
- Google (Search, Maps, Gmail, etc.)
- Facebook, Instagram, Twitter
- WhatsApp, Telegram
- YouTube
- ChatGPT

### Solutions

**Solution 1: VPN** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- Download and install VPN before arriving
- Recommended: ExpressVPN, NordVPN, Astrill
- Price: $10-15/month
- **Important**: Cannot download VPN in China

**Solution 2: International Roaming** ⭐⭐⭐
- Use your home country SIM roaming
- Direct access to all websites
- More expensive

**Solution 3: Use Chinese Alternative Apps** ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- WeChat (replaces WhatsApp)
- Baidu Maps (replaces Google Maps)
- Taobao/JD (shopping)
- Dianping (find restaurants)

## 📲 Essential Chinese Apps

### Communication & Social
1. **WeChat** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
   - Most important app
   - Chat, payment, mini-programs
   - Must download

2. **QQ**
   - Popular among young people
   - Good for file transfers

### Payment
3. **Alipay** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
   - Supports international cards
   - Essential payment tool

4. **WeChat Pay**
   - Built into WeChat
   - Widely used

### Transportation
5. **Amap/Baidu Maps** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
   - Most accurate maps in China
   - Supports English

6. **DiDi**
   - Taxi-hailing app
   - Like Uber

7. **12306**
   - Train ticket booking
   - Official app

### Lifestyle
8. **Dianping**
   - Find restaurants, read reviews
   - Like Yelp

9. **Meituan/Ele.me**
   - Food delivery
   - Group deals

10. **Ctrip/Fliggy**
    - Book hotels, flights
    - Travel booking

## 🔧 Phone Setup Tips

### iPhone Settings
1. Add Chinese App Store account
   - Download China-exclusive apps
   - Keep original account, add second account

2. Turn off iMessage/FaceTime
   - May not work in China
   - Use WeChat instead

### Android Settings
1. Install Google Play
   - Some Chinese phones don't have it preinstalled
   - May need VPN to access

2. Install Chinese app stores
   - Huawei AppGallery
   - Xiaomi App Store
   - Tencent MyApp

## ⚠️ Important Notes

### 📵 Things to Know
1. **Real-Name System**: SIM cards require passport verification
2. **VPN Compliance**: Only for personal legal use
3. **Data Monitoring**: Watch data usage, overage charges apply
4. **WiFi Security**: Be careful with public WiFi

### 💡 Useful Tips
1. Download VPN before arriving
2. Download needed Chinese apps in advance
3. Save important contacts on WeChat
4. Enable international roaming as backup
5. Screenshot important info (addresses, maps, etc.)
