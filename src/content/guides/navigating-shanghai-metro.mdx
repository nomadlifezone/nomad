# How to Navigate Shanghai Metro as a Foreigner

Shanghai's metro system is one of the longest and most efficient in the world. With 20+ lines and 500+ stations, it can seem intimidating at first—but this guide will have you navigating like a local in no time.

## Getting a Metro Card

There are two main options for paying your metro fare:

### Option 1: Single Journey Ticket
Purchase at any station's ticket machine. The machines have English interface options.

1. Select your destination station on the touchscreen
2. Insert cash or scan QR code for payment
3. Collect your ticket from the slot below

### Option 2: Shanghai Public Transportation Card (交通卡)
This rechargeable card works on metro, buses, and even some taxis. Get one at any station service counter with a refundable ¥20 deposit.

## Reading Signs and Navigation Apps

### Key Chinese Phrases

| Chinese | Pinyin | English |
|---------|--------|---------|
| 地铁 | Dìtiě | Metro |
| 入口 | Rùkǒu | Entrance |
| 出口 | Chūkǒu | Exit |
| 换乘 | Huàanchéng | Transfer |

### Recommended Apps

1. **Metro Man** — Offline metro maps, works without internet
2. **Apple Maps / Google Maps** — Both work well for metro navigation in Shanghai
3. **SH metro** — Official app with real-time arrival info

## Line-Specific Tips

### Line 2 (East-West Line)
- Connects Pudong Airport to Hongqiao Airport
- Most crowded during rush hour (8-9am, 6-7pm)
- Key stations: People's Square, Lujiazui (financial district)

### Line 10 (Northwest Line)
- Serves many expat neighborhoods
- Connects to Nanjing Road East for shopping

## Pro Tips

> 💡 **Pro tip:** During rush hour, the last carriage is often less crowded. If you're carrying luggage, avoid lines 2 and 11 during peak hours.

- Metro cards can be topped up at convenience stores (FamilyMart, Lawson, 7-11)
- Keep your card—you'll need it to exit through the turnstiles
- Trains arrive every 2-3 minutes during peak hours
- All stations have air conditioning in summer

## Emergency and Assistance

If you need help:
- Look for station staff in blue vests
- Each platform has an emergency intercom
- Police stations are available at major transfer hubs
