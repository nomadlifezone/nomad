# Complete Guide to Booking Train Tickets on 12306 China

China's high-speed rail network is the world's largest, connecting over 95% of Chinese cities with trains that routinely reach 250–350 km/h. Whether you're commuting between Beijing and Shanghai or exploring hidden gems in Yunnan, knowing how to book tickets is essential. The official platform is **12306** (12306铁路售票窗口 or yāo èr sān líng liù), operated by China Railway. This guide walks you through everything you need to book train tickets confidently—no Chinese fluency required.

---

## 📱 What You'll Need

Before you start, gather these essentials:

- **Smartphone or computer** with internet access
- **12306 app** (iOS App Store / Android APK via APKPure or official site) or website: [www.12306.cn](www.12306.cn)
- **Chinese phone number** (required for registration)
- **Valid passport** (you'll enter these details during booking)
- **Payment method**: Chinese bank card, Alipay (zhī fù bǎo), or WeChat Pay (wēi xìn zhī fù)
- **VPN** (optional but recommended if accessing from outside China)

⚠️ **Important:** The 12306 website and app are entirely in Chinese. Using a browser with built-in translation (like Chrome) or the app's simplified interface can help, but basic English fluency with the process is helpful.

## 🚗 Step-by-Step Guide to Booking Tickets

### Step 1: Download and Register on 12306

1. **Download the app** from the iOS App Store (search "铁路12306") or install via APK if your Android phone doesn't support Google Play
2. **Open the app** and tap **登录 (Login)** in the bottom right
3. **Tap 注册 (Register)** to create a new account
4. **Enter your phone number** (must be a Chinese number +86)
5. **Receive and enter the verification code** sent via SMS
6. **Set a password** (mix of letters and numbers, at least 6 characters)
7. ✅ You'll receive a confirmation—your account is ready

💡 **Tip:** If you already have a 12306 account but forgot your password, use **忘记密码 (Forgot Password)**. A code will be sent to your Chinese phone number.

### Step 2: Add Your Passenger Information

1. Tap **我的 (My)** at the bottom navigation
2. Select **乘车人 (Passengers)** → **添加 (Add)**
3. **Enter your details:**
   - Name (as shown on passport)
   - Passport number
   - Country (select from dropdown)
   - Type: **身份证 (ID Card)** or **护照 (Passport)**
4. **Confirm and save** ✅

⚠️ **Gotcha:** Your passenger information must match your passport exactly. Any discrepancy can result in being denied boarding.

### Step 3: Search for Available Trains

1. Tap **首页 (Home)** → **单程 (One Way)** or **往返 (Round Trip)**
2. **Enter your route:**
   - **出发地 (From):** Your departure city (e.g., 北京 Beijing)
   - **目的地 (To):** Your destination (e.g., 上海 Shanghai)
3. **Select your travel date** using the calendar picker
4. Tap **查询 (Search)** to see available trains

💡 **Pro Tip:** Trains on 12306 are categorized:
- **高铁 (Gāotiě):** High-speed rail (bullet trains, fastest)
- **动车 (Dòngchē):** High-speed regional trains
- **Z/T/K:** Traditional slower trains (overnight routes)

### Step 4: Choose Your Train and Seat Class

After searching, you'll see a list of available trains:

| Train Type | Speed | Best For |
|------------|-------|----------|
| G-series | 300–350 km/h | Long-distance, intercity |
| D-series | 200–250 km/h | Regional routes |
| C-series | 160–200 km/h | Suburban commuting |

1. **Tap on your preferred train** to see seat options
2. Choose your **seat class:**
   - **二等座 (Èr děng zuò):** Second class — affordable, comfortable
   - **一等座 (Yī děng zuò):** First class — more legroom
   - **商务座 (Shāng wù zuò):** Business class — maximum comfort, meals included
3. **Select a specific seat** from the available options (highlighted in blue)
4. Tap **提交订单 (Submit Order)** ✅

### Step 5: Confirm and Pay

1. **Review your order details** (date, route, seat, passenger)
2. **Select your payment method:**
   - Alipay
   - WeChat Pay
   - China UnionPay bank card
3. **Enter your payment password** (or verify via your payment app)
4. ✅ **Payment confirmed** — you'll receive an SMS with your ticket details and a **取票码 (Pickup Code)**

⚠️ **Warning:** Payment failures are common with foreign cards. WeChat Pay or Alipay is strongly recommended for foreigners.

### Step 6: Collect Your Ticket

You have two options:

**Option A — Pick up at the station:**
1. Go to the **自动取票机 (Self-Service Ticket Machine)** or **人工窗口 (Service Counter)**
2. Select **取票 (Collect Ticket)**
3. **Scan your passport** or enter your **取票码 (Pickup Code)**
4. Collect your printed ticket ✅

**Option B — Use your ticket directly (if booked with passport):**
- On high-speed trains, you can often board using just your passport and the SMS confirmation
- Go to the **快捷通道 (Express Lane)** and show your passport + SMS code

⚠️ **Note:** For cross-border routes (e.g., to Hong Kong), you'll need to collect a physical ticket before boarding.

## 💡 Tips & Common Issues

### ✅ Book During Peak Windows
Ticket availability refreshes at **6:00 AM and 6:00 PM CST** daily. Popular routes (especially Friday evenings and holiday periods) sell out fast. Set a reminder!

### ✅ Try Standby Tickets
If a route is sold out, use the **候补购票 (Standby Booking)** feature. You can join a queue and be automatically booked if seats become available.

### ⚠️ Refunds and Changes Are Strict
- **Before departure:** Tickets can be refunded or changed, but fees apply (typically ¥20 for changes, ¥15–10% of ticket price for refunds)
- **After departure:** Usually not refundable
- **No-shows:** Forfeit the ticket entirely

### ⚠️ Name Mismatch Issues
If your passport name has special characters or spaces, double-check during registration. System errors here can cause boarding rejection.

### 💡 Use English Translation Tools
The 12306 app has a built-in toggle for English in some versions. Alternatively, use Google Lens or a translation app to photograph the Chinese interface.

### 💡 Ticket Pickup Without a Phone
You can always collect tickets using your **passport number** at any ticket machine—just select 证件取票 (ID Pickup).

## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions

**Can foreigners book tickets on 12306 without a Chinese bank card?**
Yes, but you'll need either Alipay or WeChat Pay linked to a Chinese account or, in some cases, a foreign card registered on the app. WeChat Pay is often the smoother option.

**What if I don't have a Chinese phone number?**
Currently, registration requires a Chinese phone number (+86). If you're staying long-term, consider getting a SIM card from China Mobile, China Unicom, or China Telecom. For short trips, ask your hotel if you can use their phone for verification.

**How far in advance can I book tickets?**
Tickets are generally available **15 days before departure** (sometimes 20 days for holiday periods). Booking early is strongly recommended for popular routes.

**Can I cancel or change my ticket?**
Yes, through the app or at the station. Changes must be made before departure and often incur a small fee. Refunds depend on how close to departure you request them.

**Do I need to print my ticket?**
Not always. On most high-speed routes, you can board with your passport and digital confirmation. However, physical tickets are recommended as backup, especially for international travelers.

**Is there an English version of 12306?**
No official English version exists. The interface is entirely in Chinese, but the visual layout (icons, numbers) makes navigation manageable with a translation app.
