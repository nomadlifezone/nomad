# Complete Guide to Chinese Tea Culture Experience

China is the homeland of tea with thousands of years of tea culture history. This guide helps you deeply understand Chinese tea ceremony, learn tea art, and experience tea house culture.

## 🍵 Six Major Types of Chinese Tea

### 1. Green Tea ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Features**: Non-fermented, retains natural flavor, green color, fresh and refreshing

**Representative Varieties**:
- **West Lake Longjing** (Hangzhou) - Most famous ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Biluochun** (Suzhou) - Fruity aroma
- **Huangshan Maofeng** (Anhui)
- **Taiping Houkui** (Anhui)

**Brewing**: Water temp 80-85°C, 1-2 minutes, use glass cup (watch tea dance)

**Best For**: Summer cooling, refreshing, clearing heat

**Price**: ¥100-2000/50g

### 2. Black Tea ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Features**: Fully fermented, bright red tea soup, mellow taste, rich aroma

**Representative Varieties**:
- **Keemun Black Tea** (Anhui) - World's three high-fragrance teas
- **Lapsang Souchong** (Fujian) - World's first black tea
- **Dianhong** (Yunnan) - Golden buds
- **Yingde Black Tea** (Guangdong)

**Brewing**: Water temp 90-95°C, 3-5 minutes, use porcelain pot or Gaiwan

**Best For**: Autumn/winter warming, suitable for weak stomachs, pairs with Western desserts

**Price**: ¥80-1500/50g

### 3. Oolong Tea (Qing Tea) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Features**: Semi-fermented, combines green and black tea characteristics, fragrant aroma, obvious sweet aftertaste

**Representative Varieties**:
- **Tieguanyin** (Fujian Anxi) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Da Hong Pao** (Fujian Wuyi Mountain) - King of rock teas
- **Phoenix Dancong** (Guangdong Chaozhou)
- **Dongding Oolong** (Taiwan)

**Brewing**: Water temp 95-100°C, 1-3 minutes, use Yixing teapot or Gaiwan (Gongfu tea)

**Best For**: Weight loss, lowering blood lipids, tea ceremony performances

**Price**: ¥150-5000/50g

### 4. White Tea ⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Features**: Lightly fermented, natural withering, light taste, high medicinal value

**Representative Varieties**:
- **Silver Needle** (Fujian) - King of white tea
- **White Peony** (Fujian)
- **Shoumei** (Fujian)

**Brewing**: Water temp 85-90°C, 2-5 minutes, use Gaiwan or glass cup

**Best For**: Clearing heat, brightening eyes, aged tea has storage value

**Price**: ¥100-3000/50g

### 5. Dark Tea ⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Features**: Post-fermented, ages better, dark red tea soup, aids digestion

**Representative Varieties**:
- **Pu'er Tea** (Yunnan) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - Raw Pu'er: Strong taste, suitable for aging
  - Ripe Pu'er: Mild and mellow, ready to drink
- **Anhua Dark Tea** (Hunan)
- **Liubao Tea** (Guangxi)

**Brewing**: Water temp 95-100°C, rinse tea first then brew 3-5 minutes, use Yixing teapot or Gaiwan

**Best For**: Lowering fat, aiding digestion, collection and investment

**Price**: ¥80-10000+/357g (Pu'er cake)

### 6. Yellow Tea ⭐⭐⭐

**Features**: Lightly fermented, complex production, rare production, yellow soup and leaves

**Representative Varieties**:
- **Junshan Silver Needle** (Hunan)
- **Mengding Yellow Bud** (Sichuan)
- **Huoshan Yellow Bud** (Anhui)

**Price**: ¥200-2000/50g

## 🎭 Tea Ceremony Performance

### Gongfu Tea Ceremony ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Origin**: Guangdong Chaoshan, Fujian Minnan
**Features**: Refined and elegant, emphasizes tea ware and brewing skills

**Basic Process**:
1. **Prepare Tools**: Ready tea ware
2. **Warm Cups**: Warm cups with hot water
3. **Add Tea**: 1:20 ratio
4. **Rinse Tea**: First brew quickly pour out (rinse and wake tea)
5. **Brew**: High pour, low serve
6. **Divide Tea**: Evenly distribute to tasting cups
7. **Smell**: Smell aroma first
8. **Taste**: Three sips to taste

**Tea Ware**: Tea tray, Gaiwan/Yixing teapot, Fairness cup, Tasting cups, Smelling cup (optional), Six gentlemen of tea ceremony

**Suitable For**: Oolong tea, Pu'er tea

## 🎓 Tea Tasting Etiquette

### Basic Etiquette

**1. Tea Brewer**:
- ✅ Wash hands
- ✅ Clean tea ware
- ✅ Serve tea with both hands
- ✅ Guests first, host last
- ❌ Don't put fingers in cups

**2. Tea Taster**:
- ✅ Receive tea with both hands (show respect)
- ✅ Nod to show thanks
- ✅ Sip slowly (don't gulp)
- ✅ Three sips to taste (one taste, two sip, three savor)
- ❌ Don't make loud sounds

### Finger Tapping Etiquette

**Origin**: Legend says Emperor Qianlong poured tea for minister in disguise, minister couldn't kneel, used fingers to tap table representing kowtow

**Usage**:
- Elder to junior: Junior makes fist, knuckles down, tap table (bow)
- Between peers: Index and middle fingers together, lightly tap (nod)
- Junior to elder: Index or middle finger lightly tap (acknowledge)

**Modern**: When someone pours tea, tap table 2-3 times with index and middle fingers to show thanks

### Tea Tasting Steps

1. **Observe Dry Tea**: Appreciate appearance, color of dry tea
2. **Smell Dry Tea**: Smell aroma of dry tea
3. **Observe Soup**: Watch tea soup color
4. **Smell Tea**: Smell tea soup aroma (use smelling cup)
5. **Taste Tea**:
   - First sip: Small sip, feel aroma
   - Second sip: Hold in mouth, feel taste
   - Third sip: Swallow, experience sweet aftertaste
6. **Savor**: Feel throat sensation and sweet aftertaste

3. Attend tea ceremony experience class
4. Gradually try different tea types

## ⚠️ Important Notes

### Tea Drinking Taboos
- ❌ Don't drink strong tea on empty stomach
- ❌ Drink less green tea at night (affects sleep)
- ❌ Pregnant women drink less tea
- ❌ Ulcer patients shouldn't drink green tea
- ✅ Drink tea half hour after meals

### Storage Methods
- Sealed storage
- Avoid light
- Moisture-proof
- Refrigerate (green tea)

### Cultural Respect
- Respect tea master
- Keep quiet
- Don't waste tea leaves
- Feel the serenity of tea culture
