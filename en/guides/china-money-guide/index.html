How to Manage Money in China as a Foreigner

China operates almost entirely on digital payments. Cash is rarely accepted in cities, making it essential to understand how to manage your money digitally. This guide covers everything you need to know.

The Two Main Payment Apps

Alipay (支付宝)

The most popular payment platform in China. Read our full Alipay guide for setup instructions.

WeChat Pay (微信支付)

Integrated with WeChat, China’s dominant messaging app. Both apps work similarly for foreigners now.

Currency Exchange

When you first arrive, you’ll need Chinese Yuan (CNY). Here are your options:

Airport Exchanges

Convenient but typically offer poor rates. Only exchange enough to get you started.

Bank Exchanges

Better rates than airports. Most major banks (ICBC, Bank of China, HSBC) offer exchange services. Bring your passport.

ATM Withdrawals

ATMs are everywhere. Your home bank will likely charge foreign transaction fees, so check before traveling.

Tips for Saving Money

  1. Use Alipay/WeChat Pay — Many places give discounts for digital payments
  2. Check your bank’s foreign transaction fees — Consider a travel card like Wise or Revolut
  3. Use metro over taxis — Saves ¥50-100 per trip
  4. Shop at local markets — Prices are negotiable and much lower than tourist areas

Important Notes

⚠️ Credit cards are rarely accepted outside major hotels and international chains. Always carry some cash and have your payment apps set up before arriving.

💡 WeChat Pay and Alipay are now available to foreign visitors with international bank cards, though setup can be tricky. See our detailed guides for step-by-step instructions.
